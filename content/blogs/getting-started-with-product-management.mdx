## What Makes a Great Product Manager?

Product management sits at the intersection of business, technology, and user experience. The best PMs aren't just project coordinators — they're the people who answer the question: *what should we build, and why?*

## The Discovery-Delivery Loop

Every great product team runs on a tight loop between discovery and delivery:

1. **Discovery** — understanding user problems deeply before proposing solutions
2. **Prioritization** — deciding which problems to solve given constraints
3. **Delivery** — shipping incrementally and learning from real usage
4. **Retrospection** — capturing what worked and feeding it back into discovery

## Key Frameworks

### Jobs to Be Done (JTBD)

People don't buy products — they hire them to do a job. When you understand the job your user is trying to get done, you can build solutions that actually fit into their life.

### OKRs for Product Teams

Objectives and Key Results keep teams aligned on outcomes rather than output. A well-written OKR answers: *what are we trying to achieve, and how will we know we've succeeded?*

### Impact vs. Effort Prioritization

When everything feels urgent, a simple 2×2 of impact vs. effort helps separate the high-leverage work from the noise.

## The Most Important Skill

Listening. Before writing a PRD, before wireframing, before roadmapping — talk to your users. The insights that change your product usually come from a single conversation you almost didn't schedule.
