## The Adoption Problem

Most data products fail not because of bad data — they fail because no one uses them. A beautiful dashboard that lives in a bookmark nobody clicks is a $0 ROI investment regardless of the engineering effort behind it.

## Why Internal Tools Fail

- **Built for the builder, not the user** — engineers build what's easy to expose, not what operators need to decide
- **No clear job to be done** — "a dashboard for everything" is a dashboard for nothing
- **Maintenance drift** — definitions change, the tool doesn't, trust erodes

## A Better Approach

### Start with a Decision

Every data product should be anchored to a decision that someone needs to make on a recurring cadence. "How many orders did we ship?" is not a decision. "Should we increase driver incentives this weekend?" is.

### Co-design with the Consumer

Sit with the analyst, ops lead, or exec who will use the tool. Watch them do their current workflow. The gaps in their workarounds are your product requirements.

### Ship the v0 in a Spreadsheet

Before building anything, validate the logic in a spreadsheet and share it with the intended user. If they can't interpret it or find an error, your data model isn't ready.

## Metrics That Matter for Internal Products

- **Weekly active users / potential users** — adoption rate
- **Time-to-answer** — how long does it take someone to find the number they need?
- **Trust incidents** — how often does someone flag a metric as wrong?
